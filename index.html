About Me

Hello! My name is Kaya Bobo, and I am a passionate UX/UI designer dedicated to creating accessible and user-friendly designs. While I may not have professional experience in the field yet, I have worked on several projects at Kennesaw State University that have allowed me to hone my skills and develop a deep understanding of user-centered design principles.

Driven by the desire to make digital experiences accessible to all, I focus on crafting intuitive interfaces that enhance usability and inclusivity. I thrive on problem-solving, creativity, and collaboration, ensuring that every project I work on is both functional and visually engaging.

I am excited to continue growing as a designer and look forward to opportunities where I can apply my skills to real-world challenges.

Projects

In my projects, I explore the intersection of design and development, working with both Figma and coding technologies such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Some of my projects are purely design-focused, leveraging Figma to create user-friendly interfaces, while others are centered around coding, bringing designs to life with clean and efficient code.

Collaboration has been a key part of my experience, as I have worked with team members to develop projects that balance aesthetics with functionality. In several projects, I have utilized the Goal-Directed Design process, as coined by Alan Cooper. This approach has involved building wireframes, conducting usability testing, performing user interviews, and engaging in thorough research to ensure thoughtful, user-centered solutions.
